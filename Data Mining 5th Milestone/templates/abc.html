Alpha(α) & Beta(β) of Top 10 Property Stocks compared to KLSE Index
Note: Alpha(α) shows how well (or badly) a stock has performed in comparison to a benchmark index. Beta(β) indicates how volatile a stock's price has been in comparison to the market as a whole. A high alpha(α) is always good. A high beta(β) may be preferred by an investor in growth stocks but shunned by investors who seek steady returns and lower risk. An alpha(α) of 1.0 means the investment outperformed its benchmark index by 1%.
